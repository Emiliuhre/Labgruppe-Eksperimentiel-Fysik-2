\chapter{Experimental Setup}
\section{Setup}
Firstly, we use a HeNe laser as the light source in the interferometer.
We also use 2 alignment mirrors to direct and control the light into a beam splitter, that splits the light into 2 directions.
One hits a normal mirror, but the other hits a mirror mounted on a piezoelectric ring\autocite{thorlabs_pa44lew_datasheet_2024}. 
This piezoelectric is controlled by a piezo-driver\autocite{thorlabs_mdt630b_datasheet_2026}, 
which gets its signal from a function generator
