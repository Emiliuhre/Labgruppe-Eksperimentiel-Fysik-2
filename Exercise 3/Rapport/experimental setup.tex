    \caption{Setup of the Mach-Zehnder Interferometer. 
    A HeNe laser beam (red) is attenuated and aligned before entering the first beam splitter. 
    In one of the interferometer arms, the beam through an air cell, where the optical path length is increased relative to the other arm due to the higher refractive index ($n$) of the glass and pressurized air.  
    This lengthened optical path means a phase shift from the other arm, and is represented as the green colored beam. 
    The air cell has a variable refractive index $n$ depending on the pressurization. 
    When the two beams meet again their interference is shown as the dashed overlap of the beams.}
    \label{fig:setup_with_aircell}
